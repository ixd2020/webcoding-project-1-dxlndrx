Books

Salvador Dali wrote many books in his lifetime. The most popular among them are his autobiographies, The Secret Life Of Salvadir Dali and Diary If A Genius. Besides writing books, he also illustrated several books and even published a cookbook!


THE SECRET LIFE OF SALVADOR DALI

The Secret Life of Salvador Dalí describes the artist’s life from his early childhood till the outbreak of the Second World War. The book attracted a mixture of criticism and appraisal when it was first published.

DIARY OF A GENIUS

The Diary of a Genius shows how Dali draws inspiration from everything from excrement to rotten fish to compose his work, as well as talking about his love for his partner.

LES DINERS DE GALA
LES DINERS DE GALA

Dalí and Gala were known for throwing elaborate and bizarre parties. In 1941, he invited guests like Bob Hope and Alfred Hitchcock, who were asked to dress up as their own dreams. Gala wore a unicorn’s head. Dalí borrowed monkeys from the San Francisco zoo for the evening, and guests were served fish in satin shoes, followed by live frogs. In 1973, Dalí released his own cookbook, Les Diners de Gala, a how-to guide to Surrealist cooking that featured some of Dalí’s favourite motifs, like snails, lobsters, and eggs.The book was illustrated with photos of Dalí himself in front of banquets of food and his drawings.
