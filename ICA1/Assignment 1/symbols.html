Symbols

Dalí Melting Clocks

The famous melting clocks represent the omnipresence of time. They represent time devouring itself. His inspiration for melting clocks came from the surreal way where he saw a piece of cheese melting in the sun.

Dalí Ants

Ants used in Dalí’s paintings and sculptures represent death and decay, a reminder that in life, nothing is permanent. They also represent overwhelming sexual desire.

Dalí Eggs

The egg shows a contrast between its hard exterior and soft interior, just like the realities of life. Dalí links the egg to pre-natal images and the intra-uterine universe, symbolising both hope and love.

Dalí Crutches

The crutch is a symbol of tradition, upholding essential human values. It represents the realities of the real world, providing spiritual and physical support for inadequacy in life.

Dalí Elephants

Dalí’s elephants represent the future and are also a symbol of strength. The elephants have long and thin legs, carrying objects on their backs. The weight supported by the spindly legs show weightlessness, only made more significant by the burden of their backs.

Dalí Drawers

The drawers arise from their Freudian explanation as a representation of the concealed sexuality of women. Dalí portrays many of the drawers to be slightly ajar, indicating that their secrets are known and no longer to be feared
